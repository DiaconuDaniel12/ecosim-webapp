Crafting & Production

Make the economy feel alive: demand, supply, and meaningful upgrades.

Craft
Combine resources into equipment, consumables, building parts, and premium recipes. Higher tier crafting needs higher star workers.
Ship
Deliver crafted goods to contracts (NPC) or sell to players. Contracts stabilize price floors so the economy does not die.

Merge / Upgrade System

Progression that feels powerful and also keeps supply under control.

Stars & Level Caps

Every worker and building levels from 1 to 30. At level 30, fuse two identical-tier assets to create a higher star asset.

2x Level 30 -> 1x Star +1 (supply decreases)
Higher-star buildings require higher-star workers
Star tiers unlock better slots, better efficiency, and new recipes

ECO Tokenomics

Total Supply: 1,000,000,000 ECO (Solana)

ECO powers the EcoSim economy: crafting, upgrades, marketplace fees, and long-term ecosystem growth. ECO is designed for a sustainable in-game economy - emissions are controlled, major allocations are vested, and liquidity is managed responsibly. In-game points are off-chain and do not affect ECO's total supply.

Allocation

  • 30% - Game Rewards (300,000,000 ECO): earned through gameplay; emissions capped plus sinks (upgrades, crafting, fusion).
  • 22.5% - Liquidity & Listings (225,000,000 ECO): DEX liquidity and future listings; LP planned to be locked.
  • 15% - Team (150,000,000 ECO): vesting 12-month cliff plus 36-month linear unlock.
  • 7.5% - Early Adopters (75,000,000 ECO): milestone-based rewards for real contributors (anti-farm).
  • 8% - Seed Round (80,000,000 ECO): vesting 3-month cliff plus 12-month linear unlock.
  • 7% - Private Round (70,000,000 ECO): vesting 6-month cliff plus 18-month linear unlock.
  • 5% - Public Sale (50,000,000 ECO): 20-30% at TGE, remainder over 6-9 months.
  • 5% - Advisors & Partners (50,000,000 ECO): vesting 6-month cliff plus 18-month linear unlock.

In-Game Points vs ECO

In-Game Points are not ECO. Points are off-chain progression units used inside the game. ECO is the on-chain asset with fixed total supply. Any future conversion (if enabled) will be capped and rate-limited.

All allocations and schedules may be refined during development to ensure a healthy economy and fair launch conditions.

All allocations are transparent on-chain and subject to vesting schedules.

Roadmap

EcoSim is built in phases. We ship playable features first, then expand the economy, NFTs, and on-chain utilities step by step.

Phase 0 - Foundation (Now)

  • ECO token created on Solana (mint + metadata)
  • Website v1 (tokenomics, roadmap, wallet connect)
  • Core economy design (sources/sinks, crafting, upgrades, fusion)
  • Security baseline: treasury setup + vesting plan

Goal: a clean, verifiable foundation before public growth.

Phase 1 - Core Gameplay (Alpha)

  • Playable loop: build -> produce -> craft -> upgrade
  • Worker progression (levels + talents)
  • Building upgrades + fusion to star tiers (supply reduction concept)
  • Off-chain points system for progression (separate from ECO)

Goal: a fun game loop with clear progression and economy balance.

Phase 2 - Economy & Marketplace (Beta)

  • In-game marketplace (player-to-player trading)
  • Resource chains + crafting recipes expansion
  • ECO sinks: fees, repairs, upgrades, fusion costs
  • Early adopters program (milestone-based rewards, anti-farm)

Goal: a working economy where players create value and trade.

Phase 3 - On-Chain Assets (Launch)

  • NFTs for buildings, workers, and items (ownable assets)
  • Withdraw/import system (game <-> wallet)
  • Vesting enabled for key allocations (team/rounds/advisors)
  • DEX liquidity setup (with LP lock plan)

Goal: true ownership + transparent on-chain economy.

Phase 4 - Expansion & Governance

  • Seasonal events + limited drops
  • Staking / utility expansions (as economy allows)
  • DAO-ready treasury (multi-sig -> governance roadmap)
  • New regions, factories, and production layers

Goal: long-term sustainability and community-driven evolution.

Timelines may shift as we prioritize security, economy balance, and a high-quality player experience.

FAQ

Short answers, no BS.

Is this pay-to-win?
No. The focus is economy gameplay. Tokens/NFTs are utility layers. Progress comes from building good production chains and smart trading.
How do merges reduce supply?
Two level-30 assets fuse into one higher-star asset. That permanently lowers the total count of assets available in the economy.
What makes the economy decentralized?
Players set prices, demand is driven by production and crafting needs, and the market has sinks to reduce inflation. On-chain trading comes after balance.
When will on-chain start?
After core gameplay is stable and fun on mobile. Web3 only ships when it improves the game, not when it distracts from it.
